Lei is a master's student at the College of Information at the University of Maryland. Previously she completed a B.S. in Foreign Literature at Xiamen University. She aims to bridge the gap between cutting-edge AI and people with disabilities, fostering a more equitable AI environment for diverse user groups. Her research focuses on designing, developing, and evaluating prototype assistive devices for individuals with speech difficulties. Currently, She is exploring ways to improve the agency of individuals with aphasia in interacting with multimodal AI, including speech recognition, image generation, and text generation.
